# Why KIGLAND

**1. Making Kigurumi accessible, not exclusive**

Kigurumi headshells have long been a premium, high-barrier product. Market prices typically range from ¥5,000 to ¥10,000 (roughly $700–$1,400), with long wait times and unpredictable delivery. Many people never got past "I wish I could."

KIGLAND was founded in 2024 to change that. Through digital modeling, structural optimization, and supply chain improvements, we brought the starting price down to ¥998.

This isn't just about being cheaper. It means more people — not just the ones with deep pockets — can own a faithful character likeness. We believe Kigurumi should be for everyone who loves a character, not a luxury for the few.

**2. A real company with real accountability**

KIGLAND is backed by state-affiliated investment and counts Dr. Lu Qi (former Baidu COO, now MiraclePlus founder) among its investors. We're not a solo studio — we're a proper company with a team behind every order.

Why does that matter for you? Because a headshell isn't an off-the-shelf product. It involves character interpretation, aesthetic judgment, material fabrication, wear fit, and after-sales support. You need more than just a product — you need a team that communicates clearly and stays responsible through the entire process.

We've built solid service reputation among our early users, and we keep improving our ordering, production, delivery, and support workflows so every customer can start their Kigurumi journey with confidence.

**3. More experience than most, and still improving**

KIGLAND doesn't just sell headshells to end customers. We also do OEM manufacturing for other shops in the market.

What does volume get you? More character types encountered. More face shape requests handled. More fit feedback received. More edge cases to learn from. All of that feeds back into our process — where to reinforce, what to adjust, which shell shapes suit which face types better. These aren't design theories; they're lessons from actual production.

Choosing KIGLAND means choosing a team that's actually built a lot, seen a lot, and keeps getting better.
