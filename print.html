什么是 Kigurumi / What is Kigurumi

1. 一种来自日本的角色扮演方式

Kigurumi 是一种来自日本的角色扮演方式，日语写作「着ぐるみ」。这个词可以通俗理解为“把角色或娃娃穿在身上“。

它通常由角色的头壳面具、服装、覆盖全身的打底衣等部分组成，通过完整的穿戴造型，让扮演者在现实中呈现出接近动画、游戏或原创角色设定的形象。

相比单纯穿上角色服装，Kigurumi 更强调从面部到身体的整体角色感。戴上头壳、穿上服装后，玩家在镜子、镜头和活动现场中看到的是一个更完整、更接近设定的角色形象。

2. 与传统 Cosplay 的区别

Kigurumi 和传统 Cosplay 的主要区别，在于角色面部的呈现方式和材料工艺。

传统 Cosplay 通常通过化妆、假发、服装、表情管理和拍摄角度来接近角色；Kigurumi 则会使用角色头壳来呈现角色的脸。

头壳通常由树脂、玻璃钢、3D 打印材料或其他复合材料制作，再经过建模、打磨、上色、眼片装配、内衬调整等工艺完成。角色的脸型、眼睛、表情和整体可爱感，会被稳定地固定在头壳结构中，因此每次穿戴都能保持相对一致的角色效果。

3. 更稳定的还原与更低的参与门槛

Kigurumi 的优势在于稳定还原和更低的参与门槛。

许多二次元角色的眼睛比例、脸型结构、鼻口表现和现实人脸存在明显差异，仅依靠化妆和拍摄很难长期保持接近设定的效果。通过角色头壳，这些关键特征可以被直接呈现出来。

对普通玩家来说，Kigurumi 也更加友好。玩家无需特别优秀的身材、容貌或妆造能力，也可以获得接近角色设定的扮演效果。它让更多喜欢角色的人，可以更轻松地拥有一个清晰、可爱、稳定的角色形象。

4. 一种探索自我和第二身份的方式

在 KIGLAND 对大量玩家的观察和调研中，我们发现，Kigurumi 对许多人来说也是一种探索自我和第二身份的方式。

真正打动玩家的时刻，往往发生在第一次照镜子、第一次拍照、第一次以角色形象和他人互动的时候。

Kigurumi 让“喜欢一个角色“从观看、收藏、购买周边，延伸为真实的身体体验、社交体验和互动体验。玩家可以用角色的样子参加活动、记录影像、认识同好，也可以借由这个形象表达日常生活中较少展现的一面。

KIGLAND 将这种体验定义为：通过角色形象，建立属于自己的第二身份。

What is Kigurumi

1. A form of cosplay from Japan

Kigurumi (着ぐるみ) is a Japanese style of character performance. The word loosely translates to “wearing a character” — like putting on a costume that transforms you from head to toe.

A full Kigurumi setup typically includes a character mask (headshell), a bodysuit (zentai), and the character’s outfit. The idea is simple: by covering your face and body completely, you present the character as a whole, not yourself in a costume.

Unlike just wearing a character’s clothes, Kigurumi aims for a full-body transformation. When you look in the mirror or through a camera lens, what you see is the character — not “you trying to look like them.”

2. How it differs from traditional cosplay

The key difference is the face. Traditional cosplay relies on makeup, wigs, expressions, and camera angles to approximate a character. Kigurumi replaces all of that with a sculpted headshell.

The headshell itself is a serious craft. It’s typically made from resin, fiberglass, 3D-printed materials, or other composites — then modeled, sanded, painted, fitted with eye inserts, and padded for comfort. Once finished, the character’s face shape, eye proportions, and expressions are locked into the shell. No matter how many times you wear it, the look stays consistent.

3. More consistent results, lower barriers

Anime and game characters often have eye proportions and facial structures that real human faces simply don’t match. Makeup can only bridge so much of that gap.

A headshell solves this directly. The eyes are exactly the size they should be. The face shape is what it should be. You don’t need to fight your own features.

And the barrier to entry is surprisingly low. You don’t need a particular face, body type, or makeup skill. If you like a character, you put on the shell and the suit, and you’re remarkably close. That makes Kigurumi one of the most accessible forms of character performance out there.

4. A second identity, not just a costume

From our conversations with players, the real moment of conversion isn’t watching someone else’s video — it’s the first time you see yourself in the mirror as the character.

That feeling changes things. Liking a character used to mean watching, collecting, buying merch. Kigurumi extends that into something physical and social. You attend events as the character. You take photos. You meet people. You express a side of yourself that doesn’t normally come out.

At KIGLAND, we describe this as: building a second identity through the character you love.

Mirror Moment 镜面时刻 / Mirror Moment

Mirror Moment（镜面时刻）是 KIGLAND 非常重视的一种用户体验：当玩家第一次戴上 Kigurumi 头壳或完整造型，在镜子、手机前置摄像头、镜头或他人视角中看到自己时，突然意识到“我真的变成了这个角色“的那个瞬间。

这个瞬间通常很短，却很关键。它可能表现为停顿、笑出来、害羞、反复照镜子、马上想拍照，或者突然用更接近角色的姿态面对镜头。对很多玩家来说，这也是 Kigurumi 体验中最让人记住的一刻。

1. 为什么叫 Mirror Moment

在此之前，角色存在于设定图、头像、游戏、动画或想象里——是你喜欢、并期待靠近的样子。当你真正戴上头壳、穿上服装，在镜子里看到那个形象出现在自己身上时，角色第一次从想象进入现实。

它不只是“试戴效果不错“或“产品好看“，而是你第一次感受到：自己和理想角色之间的距离被缩短了。那个角色开始以你的身体、姿态和互动方式，出现在现实中。

2. Mirror Moment 通常如何发生

它往往经历四个自然阶段：

  1. 看见——通过镜子、自拍或朋友的视角，看到自己出现了不同于日常的形象。你会开始找角度、看眼睛、看脸型，在镜子前多停留一会儿。
  2. 认领——这是最关键的一步。你开始觉得镜子里的形象和自己产生了连接，它不再只是一个头壳，而是“我可以成为的样子“。当你说出“我变成她了““这就是我想要的感觉“时，Mirror Moment 往往已经发生。
  3. 进入——身体开始跟随角色：姿态更柔和、手势更可爱、拍照更愿意摆姿势，甚至说话节奏也会变化。很多时候身体先做出反应，语言才跟上。
  4. 确认——当朋友、摄影师或现场其他人用角色名称呼你、主动拍照、说“好可爱““像活了”，这个第二身份就会变得更稳定，从一个人的镜前体验延伸为可被他人看见和参与的互动。

3. 如何更容易获得 Mirror Moment

  • 用一面大镜子：全身镜更能看到头壳、服装、身体比例和姿态的整体组合。
  • 准备柔和明亮的光线：太暗会影响眼睛、脸型和涂装的呈现，自然光或柔光更适合第一次观察。
  • 给自己一点时间：第一次不必急着拍照或评价，先在镜子前看一会儿、调整角度。很多 Mirror Moment 就发生在这个停顿里。
  • 让熟悉的朋友帮你拍摄：朋友的视角更接近他人看你的方式，请他们用角色名或角色语境互动，会帮你更自然地进入状态。
  • 保存第一张照片或视频：它记录的不只是产品交付，也是你第一次看见这个第二身份的时刻。

4. 第一次没有强烈感觉，也很正常

Mirror Moment 不一定在第一次佩戴就发生。有些玩家需要适应头壳的重量、视野和呼吸感；有些要搭配完整服装、手套、假发后才进入状态；也有些是在第一次拍摄、第一次参加活动、第一次被别人叫角色名时才真正感受到连接。

这并不代表体验失败。随着穿戴次数增加，动作会更自然，姿态会更接近角色，进入第二身份也会更快。

5. Mirror Moment 与第二身份

KIGLAND 把 Kigurumi 理解为一种探索自我和第二身份的方式。这里的“第二身份“不是逃离现实，也不是要你变成另一个人，而是一种可被穿戴、可被观看、可被互动的角色化自我——你可以在这个形象里表达平时较少展现的一面。

Mirror Moment 就是第二身份第一次变得清晰的瞬间。因此它也是 KIGLAND 判断产品体验的重要标准之一：一个头壳可以在工艺上完成（脸型准确、眼睛漂亮、涂装干净），但真正重要的是戴上之后能不能让玩家产生连接、愿意拍第一张照片、愿意再次穿戴。我们希望你收到的不只是一个被制作出来的角色外观，而是一个可以被你认领、并愿意称之为“我“的形象。

What is Mirror Moment

The Mirror Moment is a key experience concept at KIGLAND — the instant when a player first sees themselves in a kigurumi headshell or full outfit and suddenly realizes: “I’ve actually become this character.”

It’s usually brief, but powerful. It might look like a pause, a laugh, shyness, repeated mirror-gazing, an urge to take photos, or instinctively shifting posture to match the character. For many players, it’s the moment they remember most.

1. Why we call it Mirror Moment

Up until then, the character lives in reference art, avatars, games, anime, or imagination — something you like and want to look like. The moment you put on the headshell and suit and see that image on yourself, the character crosses from imagination into reality.

It’s not the same as “the product looks good.” It’s the first time you perceive that the distance between you and the ideal character has collapsed — that character now exists through your body, presence, and way of interacting.

2. How it usually happens

It tends to unfold through four natural stages:

  1. Seeing — Through a mirror, selfie, or a friend’s view, you notice you look different from your everyday self. You start checking angles, eyes, face shape, and linger at the mirror.
  2. Claiming — The core step. You start feeling connected to the image; it’s no longer just a headshell but “something I could be.” When you think “I’ve become her” or “this is the feeling I wanted,” the Mirror Moment has likely already happened.
  3. Entering — Your body follows the character: posture softens, gestures become cuter, posing feels natural, even speech rhythm shifts. Often the body moves first, before the mind explains.
  4. Confirming — When friends or photographers respond in character — using the character’s name, taking photos, saying “that’s adorable” — the second identity stabilizes, going from a private mirror experience to something seen and shared.

3. How to get closer to that moment

  • Use a large mirror — a full-length one shows the full combination of headshell, suit, proportions, and posture.
  • Set up soft, bright light — dim environments hide the eyes, face shape, and paint that matter most.
  • Give yourself time — don’t rush to photograph or judge; many Mirror Moments happen in a quiet pause at the mirror.
  • Have a trusted friend take photos — their view is closer to how others see you; ask them to interact using your character’s name.
  • Save that first photo or clip — it captures not just a product delivery, but the first time you saw this second identity.

4. If it doesn’t hit right away, that’s normal

The Mirror Moment doesn’t always happen on the first wear. Some players need time to adjust to the weight, field of view, and breathing feel. Some need the full outfit — gloves, socks, wig — before it clicks. Some only feel it at their first photoshoot or the first time someone calls them by the character’s name.

This isn’t failure. With more wear time, movement becomes natural, posture aligns with the character, and entering the second identity gets faster.

5. Mirror Moment and second identity

At KIGLAND, we see kigurumi as a way to explore self and a second identity — not escaping reality or becoming someone else, but a wearable, viewable, interactive character self in which you can express sides that don’t normally surface.

The Mirror Moment is the first time that second identity becomes clear, which is why it’s one of our key experience benchmarks. A headshell can be technically complete — accurate face, beautiful eyes, clean paint — but what matters is whether you connect with it, want to take that first photo, and want to wear it again. We want you to receive not just a manufactured appearance, but an image you can claim as your own.

KIGLAND 产品系列介绍 / Product Lineup

KIGLAND 的产品系列使用 M.A.G.I.C 作为整体命名体系。它不是某一个单独产品的名称，而是 KIGLAND 对不同产品线的统一归类方式，类似 Tesla 用 S / 3 / X / Y 组成完整产品矩阵。每一个字母都对应一种产品方向，用来区分不同阶段、不同定位、不同交付方式的 Kigurumi 头壳产品。

目前，KIGLAND 面向用户主要开放的是 Type-M、Type-C、Type-G 三类产品。除此之外，Type-A（也称 Alpha）是 KIGLAND 开店初期的初代探索产品；Type-I（Industrial，工业化方向）由于供应链和制造体系仍在建设中，暂未作为正式对外产品启用。

👉 不确定怎么选？先看 如何订购 / How to Order，或直接联系客服。

1. M.A.G.I.C：KIGLAND 的产品谱系

M.A.G.I.C 代表 KIGLAND 从早期探索、标准化量产、角色定制，到未来工业化生产的完整产品路径。不同 Type 对应不同用户需求：有的适合第一次尝试 Kigurumi 的玩家，有的适合明确角色设定的定制需求，有的面向更复杂的高阶制作，也有的保留给未来更大规模的制造形态。

因此在帮助中心里，M.A.G.I.C 更适合作为“产品系列总览“，而不是和 Type-M、Type-C、Type-G 并列的某个产品。用户真正下单时，需要选择的是具体 Type。

2. Type-M：标准化平价产品

Type-M 是 KIGLAND 当前面向大众玩家推出的标准化平价产品。它采用固定款式和相对标准化的生产方式，用户可以根据肩宽、佩戴需求等信息选择合适规格。

Type-G 通常会涉及更多非标准设计，例如复杂发型、特殊脸型、异形结构、特殊材质表现，或更完整的角色外观方案。它的制作自由度更高，评估和制作流程也更复杂。具体价格、工期和制作范围，需要在提交角色资料后由客服单独评估。

Type-A（Alpha）是 KIGLAND 开店之初的初代产品，代表 KIGLAND 最早期对 Kigurumi 头壳产品的理解和尝试，包括基础结构、外观风格、佩戴方式和交付流程的早期版本。

Type-I 中的 I 代表 Industrial，即工业化方向，指向 KIGLAND 未来更高规模、更稳定供应链、更完整制造体系下的产品形态。

KIGLAND Product Lineup

KIGLAND organizes its products under the M.A.G.I.C naming system — a unified framework that categorizes different product lines by stage, positioning, and delivery method. Much like how Tesla uses S / 3 / X / Y to structure its vehicle lineup, each letter in M.A.G.I.C corresponds to a distinct product direction.

Currently, KIGLAND offers Type-M, Type-C, and Type-G to customers. Type-A (Alpha) was our earliest product generation, and Type-I (Industrial) represents a future direction that is not yet publicly available.

1. M.A.G.I.C: The Product Framework

M.A.G.I.C maps KIGLAND’s journey from early experimentation to standardized production, custom orders, and future-scale manufacturing. Each Type serves a different user: some are ideal for first-timers, some for fans with specific character requests, some for advanced or commercial needs, and some reserved for large-scale industrial production down the road.

When ordering, you choose a specific Type — M.A.G.I.C is the organizing framework, not a product itself.

2. Type-M: Standardized, Affordable

Type-M is KIGLAND’s standardized product line built for accessibility. It uses fixed models and relatively standardized production, with sizing matched to shoulder width and wear requirements.

Type-M is made for first-time kigurumi players and anyone who wants a complete character experience without the ¥5,000–¥10,000 price tag of traditional custom headshells. The goal is simple: lower the barrier so more people can own their character.

3. Type-C: Character Custom

Type-C is KIGLAND’s character-specific custom product line. It’s for players who already have a defined character and want a headshell made specifically for that design.

Unlike Type-M’s fixed models, Type-C involves modeling and production based on your character references — face shape, eyes, expression, hairstyle, and overall aesthetic. You submit character art, reference images, or original character designs, and we evaluate based on complexity, completeness, and production feasibility. Before ordering, you’ll confirm scope, pricing, timeline, and payment with our team.

4. Type-G: Advanced Custom

Type-G is for high-spec, high-complexity custom projects. This includes users with demanding accuracy requirements, complex character designs, professional exhibitions, brand collaborations, commercial projects, or special character builds.

Type-G often involves non-standard elements — complex hairstyles, unusual face shapes, non-standard structures, special materials, or full character appearance solutions. Pricing, timeline, and scope are evaluated case-by-case after submitting character reference materials.

5. Type-A / Alpha: The Original

Type-A (Alpha) is KIGLAND’s first-generation product from our earliest days. It represents our initial understanding of kigurumi headshells — the basics of structure, style, fit, and delivery.

Alpha matters because it took us from “we can make this” to “we can deliver this” and gave us our first batch of real user feedback. As our production and standards have evolved, Type-A is no longer a primary recommendation, but it’s where we started.

6. Type-I: Industrial (Not Yet Available)

The I in Type-I stands for Industrial. It represents KIGLAND’s vision for higher-scale production with a more mature supply chain and manufacturing system.

We’re still building toward that stage, so Type-I is not currently available as an external product. It’s kept in our internal roadmap but marked as “Coming Soon” in user-facing materials.

MAICRA 打底衣 / MAICRA Zentai

MAICRA「麦卡」是 KIGLAND 专为 Kigurumi / Cosplay 玩家设计的打底衣（俗称“皮“），覆盖头壳以下的全身皮肤，是完整 Kig 造型的基础部分。

MAICRA is KIGLAND’s bodysuit (“the skin”) designed specifically for Kigurumi / Cosplay players. It covers all skin below the headshell and is a core part of a complete kig look.

1. 它是什么 / What it is

打底衣是覆盖头壳以下所有皮肤的全身紧身连体衣，让扮演者从脸到身体呈现完整、统一的角色形象。MAICRA 采用接近肤色的粉色系（Pink Flesh），与头壳衔接更自然。

常见有两种款式（详见 术语表 / Glossary）：

  • 全包式：完整包裹，适合棚拍，造型最干净。
  • 开眼 / 脸窗式：露出视窗区域，是外出佩戴头壳时的常见搭配。

2. 如何选购 / How to order

打底衣的合身度直接影响穿戴效果，需要按身体数据定制：

  1. 先阅读 如何订购 / How to Order 了解整体流程。
  2. 按 测量指南 / Measurement Guide 提供身体尺寸（MAICRA 专用测量见英文版的 MAICRA Measurement Guide）。
  3. 联系客服确认款式、尺码与报价。

3. 保养须知 / Care

🙏 请让您的皮远离魔术贴！Keep your suit away from Velcro!

魔术贴的钩面会勾丝、刮花弹力面料，是打底衣最常见的损伤来源。日常保养建议：

  • 手洗、阴干，不要暴晒或机洗甩干；
  • 远离魔术贴、粗糙表面和尖锐饰品；
  • 不穿时平铺或挂置于干燥通风处，避免长期挤压。

Hand-wash and dry in shade — no machine spin or direct sun. Keep away from Velcro, rough surfaces, and sharp accessories. Store flat or hung in a dry, ventilated place.

How to Order

KIGLAND offers two types of ordering: standard models and custom builds. Which one you choose depends on whether you already have a specific character in mind, whether you like one of our existing designs, and your budget and requirements.

1. If we already made the character you love

If you have a favorite character and KIGLAND happens to have a model that matches — for example, our takes on styles inspired by characters like Hatsune Miku, Shimakaze, or similar — then Type-M is usually the best and most affordable choice.

Type-M is our standardized product line: lower price, simpler ordering, and great for first-timers. Pick an existing design, place your order, and wait for delivery.

Type-M also works well for DIY enthusiasts. After receiving the headshell, you can customize it further — swap the outfit, change accessories, re-style the wig, add small decorations, or make your own modifications. It’s an excellent entry point for anyone who wants to try Kigurumi on a budget.

2. If you want a specific character (custom)

If you have a character in mind but we don’t currently have a matching model, you’ll need to contact us for a custom consultation. Our team will evaluate based on the character’s design, production complexity, accuracy requirements, and current queue status.

To help us assess accurately, please provide clear character reference images. The ideal reference is a front-facing portrait — like a passport photo — with the face centered, features clearly visible, no hair obstructing the face, and even lighting. This helps us judge the face shape, eyes, bangs, hair color, and overall style.

Side views, turnaround sheets, expression references, concept art, or any other reference material you like are also welcome. The more complete your materials, the more accurate our evaluation. Original characters, fan designs, game characters, and anime characters are all acceptable — final acceptance depends on our team’s assessment.

3. What does the ordering process look like?

The following is a general reference. Actual steps may vary depending on product type and specific arrangements.

  1. Consultation & submit materials Contact KIGLAND customer service. Let us know if you want a Type-M standard model or a custom build. For customs, include your character references and key requirements.

  2. Evaluation & quote We’ll assess complexity, scope, materials, and our current production queue, then provide a preliminary quote and estimated timeline. Complex characters may require additional discussion.

  3. Complete measurements If your product involves a zentai bodysuit or requires body-matched sizing, we’ll provide measurement guidance. Follow the instructions carefully — your measurements directly affect the final fit.

  4. Place deposit Once the quote, direction, and measurements are confirmed, place your deposit as instructed. For custom orders, deposits are generally non-refundable once production begins. Confirm your requirements, price, and timeline before paying.

  5. Design phase begins After we confirm receipt of your deposit, design and production preparation starts. Depending on the product type, this may include 3D modeling, structural planning, hair breakdown, or color scheme confirmation.

  6. Review 3D design During the design phase, we’ll share 3D design drafts or progress renders for your review — face shape, proportions, hair structure, and other key elements. Check carefully and let us know if anything needs adjustment.

  7. Confirm hair color & details Before or during production, we’ll confirm hair color, overall tones, and certain appearance details. Actual colors may differ slightly from screen previews due to display, material, and lighting variations.

  8. Wait for production preview When the product is complete or near completion, we’ll share production preview images for your approval. Confirmation checkpoints vary by product type.

  9. Final delivery Once everything is confirmed and payment is settled, we’ll ship your product. Upon receiving it, check the packaging, labels, headshell, and accessories for completeness and any issues.

4. A few things to know before ordering

Custom kigurumi products aren’t like buying something off the shelf. They involve character interpretation, 3D design, material fabrication, handcraft work, and assembly. For the smoothest experience, prepare clear reference images beforehand and think about what matters most to you — the eyes, the face shape, the hair color, the overall cuteness, accuracy, or general vibe.

If you’re unsure whether Type-M, Type-C, or Type-G is right for you, reach out to us. We’ll help you figure out the best fit based on your budget, character needs, and how you plan to use the headshell.

产品装备 / Gear & Products

常见问题 / FAQ

关于 Kigurumi / About Kigurumi

Q: Kigurumi 到底是什么？

一种来自日本的全套角色扮演方式。戴上角色头壳面具、穿上打底衣和服装，从脸到身体完整变成角色。和传统 Cosplay 不同，不需要靠化妆去“接近“角色——头壳直接决定了角色的脸。

Q: 头壳是硬的还是软的？

绝大多数是硬壳，用玻璃钢、树脂或 3D 打印材料制作。内部会加海绵衬垫，不会直接硌到头。

Q: 戴上头壳怎么看路？

头壳的眉毛位置有视窗（细密网状结构），从外面看是二次元眼睛，从里面看是半透明的。可以看到路，但视野受限，勉强走路没问题，上下楼梯建议有后勤陪同。

Q: 会不会闷？

会的。头壳内部通风有限，夏天尤其闷热。部分型号支持安装小风扇，长时间穿戴建议每隔 20-30 分钟摘下来透气。

Q: 男生可以玩吗？

可以。Kigurumi 不挑性别。实际上，社区里相当多的玩家是男生。

关于穿戴使用 / Wearing & Using

Q: 戴头壳以后怎么交流？

有几种方式：直接说话（声音会闷）、用手机打字、用液晶手写板、或者让后勤帮忙转达。部分玩家也会用肢体动作和手势代替。

Q: 打底衣（皮）是必须的吗？

是的。打底衣覆盖头壳以下所有皮肤，是 Kig 造型的基础部分。分为全包式（棚拍用）和开眼/脸窗式（外出的标配）。

Q: 第一次戴需要注意什么？

  • 选一面大镜子，全身镜最佳
  • 光线要明亮柔和
  • 不要急着拍照，先花几分钟看镜子里的自己
  • 如果第一次没感觉，别着急——多戴几次会越来越自然

Q: 出门需要有人陪吗？

强烈建议有后勤陪同。视野受限 + 一般不说话，有人帮看路、提东西、应对路人会安全很多。

关于安全与保养 / Safety & Care

Q: 头壳有甲醛吗？

正规厂商的头壳在出厂前会经过处理。如果担心，收到后通风放置半个月再佩戴。

Q: 怎么清洁和保养？

  • 头壳表面用软布轻轻擦拭，避免用酒精等溶剂
  • 内部海绵衬垫定期通风晾晒
  • 打底衣建议手洗、阴干，不要暴晒
  • 不戴时放在干燥通风处，避免挤压变形

Q: 可以戴着睡觉吗？

不可以。头壳是硬质结构，不适合躺卧佩戴。睡眠时请取下。

About Kigurumi (EN)

Q: What exactly is Kigurumi?

A full-body character performance style from Japan. Put on a character headshell, zentai bodysuit, and outfit — and you become the character from head to toe. Unlike cosplay, you don’t “approximate” the face with makeup — the shell is the face.

Q: Is the headshell hard or soft?

The vast majority are hard shells, made from fiberglass, resin, or 3D-printed materials. The inside is lined with foam padding so it won’t press uncomfortably on your head.

Q: How do you see through the headshell?

There’s a visor mesh hidden in the eyebrow area. From outside it looks like anime eyes. From inside, it’s semi-transparent — enough to walk carefully. Field of view is limited; stairs are best done with a handler.

Q: Is it hot inside?

Yes. Ventilation is limited and summer wear can be challenging. Some models support a small internal fan. For long sessions, take a break to air out every 20–30 minutes.

Q: Can men do this?

Absolutely. Kigurumi isn’t gender-specific, and a significant portion of the community is male.

Wearing & Using (EN)

Q: How do you communicate while wearing the headshell?

Several ways: speak directly (your voice will be muffled), type on a phone, use an LCD writing tablet, or have a handler relay for you. Many players also rely on gestures and body language.

Q: Is the bodysuit (zentai) required?

Yes. The zentai covers all skin below the headshell and is a core part of the kig look. It comes in full-coverage (for studio shoots) and open-eye / face-window styles (standard for going out).

Q: Anything to keep in mind for the first wear?

  • Use a large mirror — a full-length one is best
  • Make sure the lighting is bright and soft
  • Don’t rush to take photos; spend a few minutes just looking at yourself
  • If it doesn’t click the first time, don’t worry — it gets more natural with each wear

Q: Do I need someone with me when going out?

Strongly recommended. With limited vision and usually no speaking, having a handler to watch the path, carry things, and handle passersby is much safer.

Safety & Care (EN)

Q: Does the headshell contain formaldehyde?

Reputable manufacturers treat shells before shipping. If you’re concerned, air it out in a ventilated space for about two weeks before wearing.

Q: How do I clean and maintain it?

  • Wipe the shell surface gently with a soft cloth; avoid solvents like alcohol
  • Air out the inner foam padding regularly
  • Hand-wash the bodysuit and dry in shade — no direct sunlight
  • Store in a dry, ventilated place and avoid crushing it out of shape

Q: Can I sleep in it?

No. The headshell is a rigid structure and isn’t suitable for lying down. Always take it off before sleeping.
